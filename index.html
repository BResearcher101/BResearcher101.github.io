I work at the intersection of behavioural science, digital health, and research delivery. I enjoy building collaboration around complex problems, whether I am coordinating a multi-site study, synthesising evidence, or supporting teams to work in a more transparent and replicable way.

I focus on how people adopt and sustain healthier, safer, and more sustainable behaviours. To understand what drives change, I look at what people do now, what we want to support, and what needs to shift in capability, opportunity, and motivation.

Because human behaviour is complex, I take a multidisciplinary approach that draws on psychology, social science, environmental research, technology, and management. I care about practical methods that people can use, not only theory.

I also work in open science, aiming to make research more accessible and reproducible. I currently work as Programme Manager at University of Galway. I am also the co-founder of Agape Open Science Community
