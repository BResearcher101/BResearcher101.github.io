I am a behavioural researcher and clinical study programme manager with a passion for health behaviour change, co-design, open science, and translating research into real-world impact. I enjoy building collaboration around complex problems, whether I am coordinating a multi-site study, synthesising evidence, or supporting teams to work in a more transparent and replicable way.

I currently work as the Programme Manager for the D1 Now trial at University of Galway.

I focus on how people adopt and sustain healthier, safer, and more sustainable behaviours. To understand what drives change, I look at what people do now, what we want to support, and what needs to shift in capability, opportunity, and motivation.

Because human behaviour is complex, I take a multidisciplinary approach that draws on psychology, social science, environmental research, technology, and management. I care about practical methods that people can use, not only theory.

I also work in open science, aiming to make research more accessible and reproducible.

AGAPE Open Science Community

I am one of the co-founders of AGAPE, an open science community that reflects many of the values I believe in and try to practise in my work. AGAPE is one of my proudest projects and a source of genuine joy.

Read more

AGAPE was developed to create an accessible, supportive space for researchers who want to engage with open science in a practical and inclusive way. We focus on lowering barriers to participation, sharing knowledge openly, and supporting early and mid-career researchers to build confidence, skills, and networks.

The community brings together people across disciplines, career stages, and geographies, and emphasises collaboration over competition. It also serves as a platform for open educational resources, community-led learning, and peer support.

More details about the community, its activities, and resources are available on the AGAPE website.
